# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

WordPress store owners and WooCommerce developers evaluating performance tools. They're technical enough to understand WASM and Rust claims but need those claims validated quickly and credibly. Context: mid-workday, at a desk, comparing solutions — not browsing casually. They use tools like Linear, Vercel, and Figma daily, and their aesthetic radar is calibrated to precision.

## Product Purpose

Showcase Smooth Plugins — starting with Smooth Search, the first WooCommerce search engine powered by WebAssembly. Drive adoption through technical credibility and tangible performance proof. Success means a developer or store owner reads the pitch, believes it, and installs the plugin.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, Performant, Modern.

Voice: direct and technically precise — no filler, no marketing superlatives. Shows the work rather than claiming it. Tone reference: Linear's precision. The brand earns trust through restraint and specificity, not enthusiasm.

Emotional goal: "This was built by people who actually care about performance." Quiet confidence, not bravado.

## Anti-references

- Cluttered UI: too many competing elements, no clear hierarchy, visual noise
- Too bright and popping colors everywhere: multi-color card grids with lavender, peach, blue, pink all at full saturation simultaneously — each accent dilutes the others
- Generic SaaS landing pages: hero metric template (big number + small label + gradient accent), identical icon-grid cards, glassmorphism used decoratively
- Consumer e-commerce aesthetics: warm, bouncy, high-contrast promotional design

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** A performance plugin's site must itself be fast, tight, and purposeful. Every animation earns its place. No decoration for decoration's sake.
2. **Show, don't claim.** Metrics, demos, and code over marketing superlatives. The `TARGET: WASM32-UNKNOWN` badge says more than "blazing fast" ever could.
3. **Restraint is credibility.** One accent at a time. Color is reserved for meaning, not mood. A cluttered palette reads as an untrustworthy product.
4. **Designed for the developer gaze.** The audience uses Linear, Figma, Vercel. Design to their aesthetic sensibility: precision, negative space, typographic hierarchy.
5. **Precision over polish.** Not overly smooth or rounded. Technical confidence lives in tight letter-spacing, exact grids, and monospace accents — not in gradients and glows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG Level AA. Reduced motion and high contrast already handled in global CSS. Maintain minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable with visible focus indicators.
